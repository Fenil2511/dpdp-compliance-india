## RULE 1 — SHORT TITLE AND COMMENCEMENT

These Rules are called the **Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025**.

---

## RULE 2 — DEFINITIONS

Unless the context otherwise requires:

- **(a) "Act"** — means the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (No. 22 of 2023)
- **(b) "Applicable sections"** — cross-references Sections 20 and 22 (Board member salary/terms)
- **(c) "Appropriate technology"** — any technology, including artificial intelligence, that:
  - Is technically sound, uses current best practices, and meets industry standards for the purpose; AND
  - Considers factors including the volume, sensitivity, and nature of data, and the risk of harm; AND
  - Complies with applicable Indian law including the IT Act, 2000
- **(d) "Significant Data Fiduciary criteria"** — cross-references Sections 10 and 11

## RULE 3 — NOTICE BY DATA FIDUCIARY
*(In force from 3 January 2025)*

### Purpose
Prescribes the **form and manner** of notice to be given by a Data Fiduciary under Section 5 of the Act.

### Format of Notice

The notice must:
1. Be provided **before or alongside** the request for consent
2. Contain an **itemised description** — each category of personal data and its specific purpose listed separately
3. Be **concise, clear, and easily comprehensible** — no irrelevant information, no confusing language
4. Include clear affirmative action mechanism for giving consent

### Mandatory Notice Contents (Rule 3 Checklist)

The notice must inform the Data Principal of:

| # | Element | Description |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **Personal data to be processed** | Each type of personal data, itemised |
| 2 | **Purpose** | Specific purpose for each type of data |
| 3 | **Exercise of rights** | How to exercise rights under Section 6(4) [withdrawal] and Section 13 [grievance] |
| 4 | **Complaint to Board** | Manner of lodging complaint with the Data Protection Board of India |

### Language Requirement
- Notice must be available in **English**
- Must also be available in any **Eighth Schedule language** that the Data Principal can understand
- Data Principal must be given the option to choose their preferred language

### Pre-Act Consent (Transitional Notices)
For consent obtained before commencement:
- Notice must be given **as soon as practicable** after the Act commences
- Delivery channels: email, in-app notification, or other effective digital method
- Processing may continue until Data Principal receives notice and decides whether to withdraw

### Schedule 1 — Notice Format
The Schedule to Rule 3 prescribes a structured format:
- Tabular format listing each data element and purpose
- Separate field for each processing activity
- References to rights and complaint procedures

---

## RULE 4 — CONSENT WITHDRAWAL

Supplements Section 6(4) of the Act:

- The mechanism for withdrawing consent must be **at least as easy** as the mechanism for giving consent
- If consent was given through a button click → withdrawal must also be through a button click (not buried in settings)
- Data Fiduciary must:
  - Immediately stop its own processing upon withdrawal
  - Communicate withdrawal to all Data Processors and ensure they stop

## RULE 5 — CONSENT MANAGER
*(In force from 3 January 2025)*

### Registration with the Board

A Consent Manager must:
- Register with the **Data Protection Board of India**
- Meet **technical, operational, financial, and other conditions** prescribed
- Demonstrate capacity to operate an accessible, transparent, interoperable platform

### Registration Conditions (Schedule 2)
Technical conditions include:
- Interoperability standards — must work across multiple Data Fiduciaries' systems
- Accessibility standards — usable by persons with disabilities, available in multiple languages
- Security standards — secure storage of consent records, audit trails

Financial conditions include:
- Minimum net worth / financial stability criteria
- Insurance requirements

### Consent Manager Obligations
- **Accountable to Data Principal** (not to the Data Fiduciary)
- Must maintain detailed records of: consent given, date/time, purpose, data types, modifications, withdrawals
- Must provide Data Principal with view of all consents given through the Consent Manager
- Must process withdrawal instructions promptly and communicate to relevant Data Fiduciaries

### Illustrations (from Rules)
- **Illustration 1**: P is a Consent Manager. C is a Data Principal. DF is a Data Fiduciary. C appoints P to manage consent with DF. C instructs P to withdraw consent from DF. P must communicate this to DF, and DF must stop processing. P is accountable to C, not DF.
- **Illustration 2**: C gives consent through P to DF for purpose X. DF cannot process for purpose Y — even if DF requests it through P — without C's fresh consent for Y.

## RULE 6 — SECURITY SAFEGUARDS
*(Implements Section 8(5))*

### Minimum Security Measures

Data Fiduciaries and Data Processors must implement:

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Encryption** | Encryption of personal data — at rest and in transit |
| **Access Controls** | Restrict access to authorised personnel only; role-based access |
| **Monitoring and Logging** | Log and monitor access to personal data; maintain audit trails |
| **Backup and Recovery** | Regular backups; tested recovery procedures |
| **Incident Response** | Documented plan for personal data breach response |
| **Technical Currency** | Measures must be technically sound and up-to-date |
| **Sub-processor Security** | Data Processors must implement equivalent measures; Data Fiduciary must verify |

### "Appropriate Technology" Standard
Security measures must use "appropriate technology" (defined in Rule 2(c)):
- Technically sound and uses current best practices
- Considers volume, sensitivity, and nature of personal data
- Considers risk of harm to Data Principals
- Complies with IT Act 2000 and other applicable Indian law

### Data Fiduciary — Data Processor Security Chain

**Illustration (from Rules)**:
- DF engages Data Processor P for processing services
- DF instructs P on security measures P must implement
- DF remains accountable to Data Principal C for any breach by P
- If P breaches security → DF is liable to Board for failing to ensure P's compliance
- P must notify DF immediately of any breach P discovers

## RULE 7 — INTIMATION OF PERSONAL DATA BREACH
*(Implements Section 8(6))*

### Two-Stage Notification

**Stage 1: Board Notification**

Upon becoming aware of a personal data breach, the Data Fiduciary must notify the **Data Protection Board of India** using the format in **Schedule 3**:

Required information for Board:
1. Nature of the personal data breach
2. Categories of personal data involved
3. Approximate number of Data Principals affected
4. Likely consequences of the breach
5. Measures taken or proposed by the Data Fiduciary

**Stage 2: Data Principal Notification**

Data Fiduciary must notify **each affected Data Principal**:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Language** | English OR any Eighth Schedule language the Data Principal can understand |
| **Channel** | Same channel the Data Fiduciary normally uses to communicate with the Data Principal |
| **Content** | What data was compromised + what steps the Data Principal should take |

### Timing Standard
Both notifications must be made **"as soon as reasonably practicable"** after the DF becomes aware of the breach.

> **No fixed hour window** in the Rules (unlike GDPR's 72 hours). The standard is reasonable practicability. However, the Board may issue guidance on expected timelines. Best practice: target Board notification within 72 hours, DP notification within 7 days.

### Data Processor Breach Reporting
- Data Processors must notify the **Data Fiduciary** immediately upon discovering a breach
- The Data Fiduciary then has the notification obligation to the Board and Data Principals

### Schedule 3 — Breach Intimation Format
The Schedule prescribes the form for:
- (A) Board notification — structured fields
- (B) Data Principal notification — plain language template

## RULE 8 — PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA OF CHILDREN
*(Implements Section 9)*

### Verifiable Parental Consent

Before collecting personal data from a child, the Data Fiduciary must:

1. **Determine** whether the user is a child (under 18)
2. **Verify** that the consenting person is actually the child's parent/guardian
3. **Obtain** verifiable parental consent — self-declaration alone is insufficient

### Age Verification Standards
Data Fiduciaries must implement reasonable technical/organisational measures:
- Mechanism to determine whether a prospective user is under 18
- Must be proportionate to the nature of the service and data being processed
- Cannot be easily circumvented by children

### Parental Verification Mechanism
- Must verify the identity of the person claiming to be the parent/guardian
- Approved methods: to be prescribed / accepted technological verification methods
- Cannot rely solely on a checkbox or self-declaration

### Prohibitions (Even with Parental Consent)
- No behavioural monitoring/tracking of children
- No targeted advertising directed at children
- No processing likely to cause detrimental effect on child's well-being

### Exemptions (To Be Notified by Government)
Central Government will notify categories exempt from parental consent, expected to include:
- Health and welfare services for children
- Educational services
- Government services for child welfare

### Schedule 4 — Children's Data Standards
Prescribes verification mechanisms and standards for:
- Age verification
- Parental/guardian identity verification
- Exempt categories

## RULE 9 — SIGNIFICANT DATA FIDUCIARY OBLIGATIONS
*(Implements Section 10)*

### Data Protection Officer (DPO)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Who** | An individual (not a company/firm) |
| **Location** | Must be based in India |
| **Reporting Line** | Responsible to the Board of Directors (or equivalent governing body) |
| **Role** | Point of contact for grievance redressal under the Act |
| **Publication** | DPO's name and contact details must be published on the SDF's website |
| **Authority** | DPO must have adequate authority and resources to perform functions |

### Independent Data Auditor

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Independence** | Must be independent of the SDF |
| **Qualifications** | As prescribed in Schedule 5 |
| **Scope** | Full compliance evaluation against the Act and Rules |
| **Frequency** | Periodic — at minimum annual; more frequently for high-risk activities |
| **Report** | Submitted to SDF Board of Directors and available to Board on request |

### Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

Must be conducted for:
- Every **new processing activity** before commencement
- Every **material change** to existing processing
- Any processing activity **identified as high risk** by the Board

DPIA must include:
1. Description of the processing activity and its purpose
2. Assessment of the necessity and proportionality of the processing
3. Description of risks to the rights of Data Principals
4. Measures proposed to address the risks
5. Consultation with stakeholders (where feasible)

DPIA report must be:
- Maintained internally
- Submitted to the Board on request
- Updated when processing activities change

### Algorithmic Accountability

SDFs using algorithms to make decisions affecting Data Principals must:
- Describe the logic involved in automated decisions
- Disclose meaningful information to Data Principals about automated decisions
- Ensure ability to review/contest automated decisions (per Rules)

### Schedule 5 — SDF Obligations Detail
Prescribes:
- DPIA template
- Auditor qualifications and independence standards
- DPO competency standards
- Algorithmic accountability framework

## RULE 10 — DATA PRINCIPAL RIGHTS PROCEDURES
*(Implements Sections 11–13)*

### Right to Information (Section 11) Procedure

1. Data Principal submits request (form/manner prescribed)
2. Data Fiduciary must respond within **prescribed period** (to be notified)
3. Response format: structured, clear, in the language preferred by the Data Principal
4. Must include: confirmation of processing, summary of data held, list of entities data shared with

### Right to Correction/Erasure (Section 12) Procedure

1. Data Principal submits request (form/manner prescribed)
2. Data Fiduciary must act within **prescribed period**
3. For corrections: must also instruct Data Processors to correct
4. For erasure: must also instruct Data Processors to erase
5. Refusal (legal retention grounds): must inform Data Principal of reason

### Right to Grievance Redressal (Section 13) Procedure

1. Data Principal submits grievance to Data Fiduciary
2. Grievance acknowledged within **prescribed period**
3. Grievance resolved or outcome communicated within **prescribed period**
4. If unresolved/unsatisfied → Data Principal may approach the Board
5. Contact details of Grievance Officer must be published on DF's website

### Schedule 6 — Rights Request Format
Prescribes:
- Request form templates for each right
- Response timelines
- Response format standards

## RULE 11 — NOMINATION (Section 14)

- Form and manner for nominating another person prescribed
- Nominee can exercise all Data Principal rights after death or incapacity
- Nomination can be updated or revoked by the Data Principal

## RULE 12 — DATA PRINCIPAL DUTIES (Section 15)

Central Government may prescribe penalties for:
- Filing false or frivolous complaints/grievances
- Impersonation
- Suppression of material information
- Providing false particulars

## RULE 13 — SIGNIFICANT DATA FIDUCIARY NOTIFICATION PROCESS

The process for Central Government to notify an SDF:
1. Identification based on SDF criteria (Section 10(1))
2. Consultation with the proposed SDF
3. Publication in the Official Gazette
4. SDF given reasonable time to comply with additional obligations

---

## RULE 14 — DATA AUDITOR (Section 10(2)(b))

### Auditor Qualifications
- Must meet prescribed qualifications (Schedule 5)
- Must be independent of the SDF
- Must not have a conflict of interest

### Audit Process
1. Auditor evaluates compliance with the Act and Rules
2. Auditor issues audit report with findings and recommendations
3. Report submitted to SDF's Board of Directors
4. Report available to Data Protection Board on request
5. SDF must address audit findings within prescribed timeframe

### Audit Scope
- Consent notice adequacy
- Consent management mechanisms
- Security safeguards (Rule 6 compliance)
- Data Principal rights mechanisms
- Breach notification procedures
- Data retention and deletion practices
- Children's data handling
- Cross-border transfer compliance
- DPIA completion (for high-risk activities)

## RULES 15–17 — BOARD COMPOSITION AND APPOINTMENT

### Rule 15 — Chairperson and Member Qualifications
Candidates must have expertise in:
- Law, administration, technology, or data governance
- Demonstrated integrity and standing

### Rule 16 — Appointment Process
*(In force from 3 January 2025)*
- Search Committee formed by Central Government
- Transparent selection process
- Appointment by Central Government after Search Committee recommendation

### Rule 17 — Terms and Conditions
- Salary, allowances, and service conditions prescribed
- Fixed tenure (not permanent civil servants)

## RULES 18–20 — BOARD OPERATIONS

### Rule 18 — Digital Office of the Board
- All Board proceedings conducted through a digital platform ("Digital Office")
- Complaints filed online
- Notices and orders issued digitally
- Hearings conducted through video conferencing
- Records maintained digitally

### Rule 19 — Complaint Procedure
1. Data Principal exhausts Data Fiduciary grievance mechanism
2. If unresolved → files complaint through Board's Digital Office
3. Complaint includes: nature of grievance, DF details, evidence of prior grievance with DF
4. Board acknowledges and assigns reference number
5. Board examines complaint and determines sufficient grounds
6. If sufficient grounds → notice to Data Fiduciary → opportunity to respond → inquiry
7. Board passes order → notified to parties

### Rule 20 — Inquiry Procedure
- Board issues notice to respondent (DF/person under inquiry)
- Respondent given opportunity to be heard
- Board may require documents, data, records
- Board may conduct site visits (virtual or physical)
- Board records reasons for all decisions

---

## RULES 21–23 — VOLUNTARY UNDERTAKING AND ADR

### Rule 21 — Voluntary Undertaking (Section 31)

- Person may offer voluntary undertaking at any stage of proceedings
- Must be in writing; filed through Digital Office
- Board evaluates: adequacy, sincerity, and completeness
- If accepted: proceedings closed; undertaking conditions monitored
- If breached: penalty as if the original violation was proven (plus breach of undertaking)

### Rule 22 — Alternate Dispute Resolution
*(In force from 3 January 2025)*
- Board may refer matters to ADR (mediation/conciliation)
- ADR facilitator from Board-approved panel
- ADR process time-bound
- If ADR fails → matter returns to Board inquiry

### Rule 23 — Appeal to TDSAT
*(In force from 3 January 2025)*
- Form and fee for appeal prescribed
- Filed within 60 days of Board order
- TDSAT follows its existing Rules + DPDP-specific procedure
- Electronic filing available
