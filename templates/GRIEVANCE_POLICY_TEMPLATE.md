## 1. INTRODUCTION

**[Organisation Name]** ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the
personal data of every Data Principal and ensuring that any concerns about how
we handle personal data are resolved promptly and fairly.

This policy describes how we handle grievances under the **Digital Personal Data
Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)** and **Rule 10 of the DPDP Rules, 2025**.

## 3. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE DPDP ACT 2023

Before raising a grievance, you may wish to exercise the following rights:

| Right | What You Can Ask For | Section |
|-------|---------------------|---------|
| **Right to Information** | Confirmation of processing; summary of data held; who data was shared with | Section 11 |
| **Right to Correction** | Correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated data | Section 12 |
| **Right to Erasure** | Deletion of personal data when purpose is served or you withdraw consent | Section 12 |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Stop processing based on your consent | Section 6(4) |
| **Right to Nominate** | Nominate someone to exercise your rights in case of death or incapacity | Section 14 |

**How to exercise rights**: [Link to rights request portal / form / email]

If you have exercised a right and are dissatisfied with our response, you
may raise a grievance under this policy.

## 6. GRIEVANCE HANDLING PROCESS

### Step 1 — Acknowledgement
We will acknowledge your grievance within **[48 hours / 2 business days]** of receipt.
Your acknowledgement will include:
- Reference number for tracking
- Name of the person handling your grievance
- Expected resolution timeline

### Step 2 — Investigation
Our Privacy Team will investigate your grievance:
- Review the personal data and processing concerned
- Consult relevant business teams
- Review our obligations under the DPDP Act 2023

### Step 3 — Resolution
We will communicate our decision within **[30 days / [X] business days]** of
acknowledgement.

Our response will clearly state:
- Our findings
- The action we have taken or will take
- If we cannot fulfil your request — the reasons, including any legal basis for retention

### Step 4 — Appeal (If Unsatisfied)
If you are not satisfied with our resolution:
1. You may escalate to our **[DPO / Senior Grievance Review Committee]** at [email]
2. The escalation will be reviewed within **[15 business days]**

## 7. ESCALATION TO THE DATA PROTECTION BOARD OF INDIA

If after exhausting our grievance process you remain unsatisfied, you have the
right to file a complaint with the **Data Protection Board of India (DPBI)**.

**Important**: Under Section 13 of the DPDP Act, you **must first exhaust** our
grievance mechanism before approaching the Board.

**Data Protection Board of India:**
Website: [https://www.meity.gov.in/DigitalPersonalData — update with Board portal URL]

## 9. TYPES OF GRIEVANCES WE HANDLE

We handle grievances relating to:
- Processing of your personal data without valid consent or legitimate use
- Failure to provide accurate information about data processing
- Failure to correct/complete/update personal data
- Failure to erase personal data when requested
- Failure to stop processing after consent withdrawal
- Concerns about data security or a personal data breach affecting you
- Concerns about sharing of your data with third parties
- Concerns about processing of your children's data
- Any other concern about how we handle your personal data

---

## 10. WHAT WE NEED FROM YOU

To process your grievance efficiently, please provide:

1. **Your identity**: Name, registered email/phone/account ID
2. **Clear description of the grievance**: What happened? When did it happen?
3. **What you are seeking**: What outcome do you want?
4. **Relevant documents**: Any correspondence, screenshots, notices relevant to your grievance
5. **Prior attempts**: Have you already raised this with us through another channel?

---

## 11. COMPLAINTS THAT ARE NOT COVERED

We will not be able to process grievances that:
- Are **anonymous** (we cannot investigate or respond without your identity)
- Are **false, frivolous, or vexatious** (Section 15 of DPDP Act — false complaints may attract penalties)
- Relate to personal data processing that is **not by [Organisation Name]**
- Are **duplicate** of a pending grievance (we will consolidate)
- Have already been **decided by the Board** or a court
