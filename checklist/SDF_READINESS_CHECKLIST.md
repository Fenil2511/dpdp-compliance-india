# Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF) Readiness Checklist
## Section 10 of DPDP Act 2023 + Rule 9 of DPDP Rules 2025

> **Who needs this**: Any organisation that processes large volumes of personal
> data, sensitive personal data, or data that impacts national security / electoral
> democracy — and may be notified as an SDF by the Central Government.
>
> **Key principle**: Even if not yet notified, prepare NOW. The notification
> process may give limited time to comply.

---

## AM I LIKELY TO BE NOTIFIED AS AN SDF?

Score yourself on the SDF criteria (Section 10(1)):

| Criterion | Your Score (1–5) | Weight |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Volume**: Do you process personal data of 1 crore+ Data Principals? | | High |
| **Sensitivity**: Do you process health, financial, biometric, or children's data at scale? | | High |
| **Sovereignty risk**: Could a breach in your data affect India's sovereignty or integrity? | | Very High |
| **Electoral risk**: Could your data be misused to affect electoral democracy? | | Very High |
| **Security risk**: Could compromise of your data affect national security? | | Very High |
| **Public order risk**: Could a breach in your systems affect public order? | | High |

**High SDF probability** if you are in:
- **Critical Information Infrastructure (CII)**: Power, telecom, banking, healthcare, transport
- **Large social media platforms**: 5 crore+ Indian users
- **Large fintech/payment platforms**: 1 crore+ transactions per month
- **Large e-commerce**: 50 lakh+ active users
- **Health data aggregators**: Processing health data of lakhs of patients
- **Government service intermediaries**: Processing Aadhaar, PAN, voter data at scale
- **Large ed-tech platforms**: Processing data of crores of students

### DPO Competency Requirements

Ideal DPO candidate should have:
- Knowledge of DPDP Act 2023 and Rules 2025
- Understanding of RBI/SEBI/sectoral regulations (for financial sector)
- Experience in data governance or privacy management
- Familiarity with ISO 27001/27701 (preferred)
- Ability to communicate with Board, regulators, and operational teams

| 3.10 | **DPIA register** maintained (list of all DPIAs conducted, status, review dates) | | |

### DPIA Mandatory Elements (Per Rule 9)

Every DPIA must include:

1. **Description of processing activity**
   - What personal data
   - Who it belongs to (Data Principal categories)
   - How it will be processed
   - Why it is being processed (purpose)

2. **Necessity and proportionality assessment**
   - Is the processing necessary for the purpose?
   - Is there a less privacy-invasive alternative?
   - Is the amount of data proportionate?

3. **Risk assessment to Data Principals' rights**
   - Risk of breach (likelihood + impact)
   - Risk of misuse
   - Risk of discrimination
   - Risk of harm to children (if applicable)

4. **Mitigation measures**
   - Technical controls
   - Organisational controls
   - Legal safeguards

5. **Consultation** (where feasible)
   - Consultation with Data Principals or their representatives
   - DPO opinion

### DPIA Trigger Checklist

Conduct a DPIA when processing involves:
- [ ] Large scale processing of sensitive personal data (health, financial, biometric)
- [ ] Processing of children's personal data
- [ ] Systematic profiling or behavioural tracking
- [ ] Automated decision-making with significant effects on individuals
- [ ] Cross-border transfer of personal data
- [ ] Processing of personal data using new technologies (AI, ML, biometrics)
- [ ] Processing that could lead to physical, financial, or reputational harm
- [ ] Processing of personal data of vulnerable individuals
- [ ] Any new processing activity (mandatory for SDFs)
