# DPDP Data Processor Agreement (DPA) Checklist & Clause Library
## For Vendor Onboarding and Third-Party Risk Management

> Use this checklist to review existing vendor contracts and draft new DPAs.
> Every vendor/third party who receives or processes personal data on your behalf
> is a Data Processor under the DPDP Act and requires a written DPA.

## SECTION 2 — VENDOR RISK TIER CLASSIFICATION

Before executing a DPA, classify your vendor:

| Tier | Personal Data Access | Criticality | Required Due Diligence |
|------|---------------------|------------|----------------------|
| **Tier 1 — Critical** | Direct access to large volumes or sensitive personal data; hosted customer-facing systems | Business-critical; long-term | Full DPA + Security Assessment + ISO 27001 certification check + Annual VAPT report review + Audit right exercised annually |
| **Tier 2 — High** | Access to personal data as part of service delivery; moderate volume | Important; medium-term | Full DPA + Security Questionnaire (VSAQ or equivalent) + VAPT report review + Annual review |
| **Tier 3 — Medium** | Limited access to personal data (e.g., HR SaaS, travel booking) | Standard; standard term | DPA + Self-assessment questionnaire + Biannual review |
| **Tier 4 — Low** | Incidental or no access to personal data (e.g., office supplies) | Low | Standard contract DPDP clause + Annual review |

## SECTION 3 — KEY DPA CLAUSES (READY TO USE)

### Clause A — Processing Restriction (Paste into Contract)

> "The Data Processor shall process personal data only on documented instructions
> from the Data Fiduciary, for the purposes specified in Schedule [X] of this
> Agreement, and shall not process personal data for any other purpose, including
> for its own commercial purposes, product improvement, or training of artificial
> intelligence models, without the prior written consent of the Data Fiduciary.
> The Data Processor acknowledges that it acts as a Data Processor (as defined
> under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023) and not as a Data
> Fiduciary in respect of the personal data processed under this Agreement."

### Clause B — Security Safeguards (Paste into Contract)

> "The Data Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and
> organisational security measures for the protection of personal data processed
> under this Agreement, including at a minimum: (a) encryption of personal data
> at rest and in transit using industry-standard algorithms; (b) role-based access
> controls limiting access to authorised personnel only; (c) comprehensive access
> logging and audit trails; (d) regular automated and manual backups with tested
> recovery procedures; and (e) documented incident response procedures. Such
> measures shall comply with Rule 6 of the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules,
> 2025, and shall be at least equivalent in standard to those implemented by the
> Data Fiduciary. The Data Processor shall review and update these measures at
> least annually and upon any material change to its processing activities."

### Clause C — Breach Notification (Paste into Contract)

> "In the event of a personal data breach (as defined under Section 2 of the
> Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023) affecting personal data processed
> under this Agreement, the Data Processor shall: (a) notify the Data Fiduciary
> by telephone AND in writing within FOUR (4) hours of becoming aware of the
> breach; (b) provide in the notification: the nature of the breach, categories
> and approximate number of Data Principals affected, likely consequences, and
> measures taken or proposed; (c) cooperate fully with the Data Fiduciary in
> fulfilling the Data Fiduciary's notification obligations to the Data Protection
> Board of India and affected Data Principals under Rule 7 of the DPDP Rules,
> 2025; (d) preserve all evidence of the breach; and (e) not notify any third
> party (including regulatory authorities) of the breach without the prior written
> consent of the Data Fiduciary, unless independently required to do so by law."

### Clause D — Deletion on Termination (Paste into Contract)

> "Upon expiry or termination of this Agreement for any reason, or upon written
> request by the Data Fiduciary at any time, the Data Processor shall: (a) at
> the Data Fiduciary's election, either securely delete or return all personal
> data processed under this Agreement, including all copies thereof held by the
> Data Processor or its sub-processors; (b) ensure such deletion is performed
> using a secure deletion method that renders the data unrecoverable; (c) provide
> the Data Fiduciary with written certification of deletion, including the deletion
> method used and the date of deletion, within ten (10) business days of
> completing deletion; and (d) ensure all sub-processors perform equivalent
> deletion and provide equivalent certification."

## SECTION 4 — DPDP VENDOR ASSESSMENT QUESTIONNAIRE (Quick Version)

Send to all Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors. Score: Yes=2 / Partial=1 / No=0

| # | Question | Score |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | Does your organisation have an appointed Data Protection Officer or Privacy Officer? | |
| 2 | Is your organisation certified under ISO 27001:2022 or equivalent? | |
| 3 | Do you encrypt personal data at rest and in transit? | |
| 4 | Do you maintain access logs for systems processing personal data? | |
| 5 | Do you have a documented personal data breach response procedure? | |
| 6 | Can you notify us within 4 hours of discovering a personal data breach? | |
| 7 | Do you restrict sub-processor access to personal data without client approval? | |
| 8 | Do you securely delete personal data upon contract end (with certification)? | |
| 9 | Do you conduct annual VAPT (vulnerability assessment and penetration testing)? | |
| 10 | Are all staff with personal data access trained on data protection? | |
| 11 | Do you support our right to audit your DPDP compliance? | |
| 12 | Do you have a data retention schedule and enforce it? | |

**Score Interpretation:**
- **20–24**: Low risk — proceed with DPA
- **14–19**: Moderate risk — address gaps before onboarding; monitor closely
- **8–13**: High risk — require remediation plan; additional scrutiny
- **0–7**: Very high risk — do not onboard until significant improvements demonstrated
